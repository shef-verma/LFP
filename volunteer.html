Volunteer With Us

At the Lexington Food Pantry, our volunteers are the heart of everything we do. From preparing grocery orders to delivering them to families in need, we rely on the generosity, energy, and dedication of our volunteers to run our organization effectively. Whether you're a student looking to earn community service hours or an adult looking to give back to the community, there are many ways to get involved and make a difference.

Volunteer Opportunities

High School Volunteers

For Lexington High School students

For all Lexington High School students, the LFP offers a volunteer program where you can earn community service hours by helping run carts and hand out orders to our clients.

  • Volunteers must be of high school age
  • Community service hours are available year-round for Lexington residents
  • Tasks include assisting clients, organizing orders, and helping our team run smoothly on Saturday mornings
Sign Up to Volunteer

Adult Volunteers

Packaging & Front-End Support

Adult volunteers are critical to keeping the pantry running and can contribute in a variety of ways:

  • Help prepare grocery orders for families
  • Manage front-end operations, such as registering clients
  • Oversee high school volunteers
  • Current need: Registration Coordinator (greet clients, manage order distribution)
  • Requires a one-year commitment

Delivery Drivers

Help us reach those in need

Our delivery drivers play a crucial role in ensuring food reaches those who cannot visit the pantry in person.

  • Deliver grocery orders to 3-6 families each Saturday afternoon
  • Commitment: 12:30 PM - 1:30 PM on Saturdays
  • Must have a car and valid driver's license
  • Flexible scheduling options available

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the time commitment for volunteering?

Most volunteer shifts are 2-3 hours on Saturday mornings. High school volunteers typically serve 2-3 hour shifts, while adult volunteers may have longer commitments depending on their role. Delivery drivers typically commit to 1-hour shifts on Saturday afternoons.

Do I need any special training or experience?

No prior experience is necessary! We provide all the training you'll need for your volunteer role. For specific positions like Registration Coordinator, we offer more comprehensive training.

Can I volunteer if I don't live in Lexington?

While we prioritize Lexington residents for some roles, we welcome volunteers from surrounding communities, especially for our delivery driver positions.

How do I track my community service hours?

We provide documentation for all community service hours. High school students will receive a form to track their hours each time they volunteer.
