Volunteer FAQ

Answers to common questions about volunteering with us

What are the age requirements for volunteering?

Our general volunteer age minimum is 14 years old. Volunteers aged 14-15 must be accompanied by a parent or guardian. Some positions like driving for our home delivery program require volunteers to be at least 18 with a valid driver's license. Our community garden welcomes families with children as young as 10 when supervised by an adult.

Do I need any special skills or experience?

No special skills are required for most positions! We provide all necessary training. The most important qualities are compassion, reliability, and a willingness to help. For certain roles like food sorting or garden work, physical ability to lift 20-30 pounds may be needed.

What should I wear when volunteering?

Wear comfortable, weather-appropriate clothing that you don't mind getting a little dirty. Closed-toe shoes are required for all positions. For food distribution roles, we'll provide gloves and aprons. Garden volunteers should bring sun protection and consider bringing gardening gloves.

Can I fulfill community service hours with you?

Yes! We regularly work with students needing service hours for school and individuals completing court-ordered community service. Please bring any necessary paperwork with you to be signed by our volunteer coordinator. Note that we cannot accept volunteers with violent offenses due to the nature of our work.

How do group volunteer opportunities work?

We welcome groups from schools, businesses, faith organizations, and community groups. Groups of 5-15 people can typically be accommodated with advance notice. Popular group activities include food sorting, garden work, and special projects. Please contact our volunteer coordinator at least 3 weeks in advance to schedule a group opportunity.
