About Us

Our story, mission, and impact in Lexington

Our History

Founded in 1982 by a coalition of local churches, the Lexington Food Pantry began as a small closet operation serving 15 families. Today we operate a full-service distribution center serving over 1,200 residents monthly.

1982

Pantry founded by First Baptist Church members

1995

Moved to current location with dedicated space

2010

Launched home delivery program

2020

Pandemic response served triple the typical families

Our Mission

To alleviate hunger in Lexington by providing nutritious food with dignity, while building community connections and advocating for systemic change.
