About Lexington Food Pantry

Learn about our mission, history, and the community we serve

Our Mission

The Lexington Food Pantry (LFP) is a completely volunteer-run organization dedicated to ensuring that all residents of Lexington have access to nutritious food and essential household products. Our mission is to support community members facing food insecurity, providing them not only with groceries but also with care, and a sense of belonging.

Every Saturday morning from 9AM to 2:00 PM, our volunteers come together to serve over 200 families, carefully preparing and distributing orders that include staple groceries, fresh fruits and vegetables sourced from local farms, and household necessities.

"At LFP, we believe that access to healthy food is a fundamental right, and we strive to create a welcoming environment for everyone who walks through our doors."

Beyond simply providing food, we aim to foster connections within the community, offering support and resources to help families thrive. Our volunteers, who come from all walks of life, are the heart of our operations, ensuring that every client feels seen, respected, and cared for.

Our History

From Humble Beginnings

The idea for the Lexington Food Pantry originated in the early 1990s when the Lexington Council of Churches formed a study group to assess the town's need for a food support program. The group's research confirmed that many residents could benefit from additional resources, and in November 1990, the Lexington Food Pantry was born.

Initially, LFP operated out of a small basement room at the Church of Our Redeemer on 6 Meriam Street, serving a modest number of families with limited resources.

Over the years, the pantry has grown significantly to meet the increasing needs of the community. Today, LFP serves over 1,000 individuals every week, with more than 200 families receiving carefully prepared food orders.

This growth is a testament to the generosity and commitment of the Lexington community. We are deeply grateful for the ongoing support of local businesses, faith organizations, and community groups, who contribute both financially and through food donations.
