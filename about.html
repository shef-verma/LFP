Our Story

For over 40 years, we've been serving the Lexington community with dignity and compassion

Our History

The Lexington Food Pantry was founded in 1982 by a small group of concerned citizens who noticed neighbors struggling to put food on their tables. What began as a modest operation in the basement of a local church has grown into one of Lexington's most vital community resources.

In our early years, we served just 15-20 families each month, relying entirely on donations from local congregations and community members. As needs grew throughout the 1980s and 1990s, so did our operations, moving to larger spaces and establishing partnerships with regional food banks.

1982

Humble Beginnings

Founded by Reverend Thomas Wilson and six community volunteers, operating out of St. Michael's Church basement every other Saturday.

1995

First Permanent Home

Moved to our first dedicated space on Maple Street, allowing us to expand to weekly distributions and serve over 100 families monthly.

2008

Community Partnership

Formed strategic partnerships with three local supermarkets and five farms, dramatically increasing our fresh food offerings.

2015

Current Location

Moved to our current facility on Main Street, featuring refrigeration, more storage, and a welcoming client-choice model.

2020

Pandemic Response

Adapted operations to safely serve record numbers during COVID-19, including launching our home delivery program.

Today, we serve over 1,200 families each month with the help of more than 200 dedicated volunteers. While our operations have grown and evolved, our core mission remains the same: to provide nutritious food with dignity and compassion to our Lexington neighbors in need.

Our Mission

To alleviate hunger in Lexington by providing access to nutritious food in a respectful manner, while working with the community to address the root causes of food insecurity through education, advocacy, and collaboration.
